Align-Items Property

The align-items property can move the flexbox items around inside of a parent container such as a div element along the cross axis.

For flex-direction equal to row, this will line up flex items top to bottom.

For flex-direction equal to column, this will line up flex items left and right. Below, is an example of the axis for flex-direction set to row.

Below are the align-items property values:

  • Normal
  • Stretch
  • Center
  • Flex-start
  • Flex-end

Normal Value

This is the default behavior of the align-items property when used within the flexbox container. The items start at the top left and move from left to right to form a "row". The items will stretch the items to fill the container along the cross axis.

Also, note that the 'normal' and 'stretch' values will fill the entire cross axis no matter how big the flex container is.
